\section{Printing a Document}
\label{sec:printing}
\index{MS Word!printing}

\begin{labstep}[How to Print]
\begin{enumerate}[label=\protect\stepcircle{\arabic*}, leftmargin=2cm, itemsep=3pt]
  \item Press \key{Ctrl}+\key{P} or go to File $\rightarrow$ Print.
  \item The \textbf{Print Preview} shows how your document will look on paper.
  \item Choose the number of copies.
  \item Select your printer from the list.
  \item Click \textbf{Print}.
\end{enumerate}
\end{labstep}

\begin{notebox}
Always check the \textbf{Print Preview} before printing! This prevents wasting paper on a document that doesn't look right. Think green --- save trees! \faTree
\end{notebox}

\bytetip[warn]{Before printing, make sure a printer is connected and turned on. If you see ``Printer Offline,'' check the cable, Wi-Fi connection, or ask your teacher for help.}

\begin{funfact}
The average office worker uses about \textbf{10,000 sheets} of paper per year. That's equivalent to one whole tree! Using Print Preview and printing only what you need helps save the environment.
\end{funfact}
