\bytetip[tip]{Your computer lab is a special place --- treat it with care, and it (and your body!) will thank you. Let's learn how to be a safe and responsible computer user!}

% ---------------------------------------------------------------
\section{Why Do We Need Lab Rules?}
\label{sec:why-rules}
\index{computer lab!rules}

A computer lab is full of expensive equipment that can be easily damaged. The rules exist to:
\begin{itemize}[leftmargin=*, label={\textcolor{ModuleBlue}{\faAngleRight}}, itemsep=2pt]
  \item Keep \textbf{you} safe from electrical hazards and health problems
  \item Keep the \textbf{computers} safe from food spills, rough handling, and viruses
  \item Make the lab a \textbf{pleasant place} for everyone to work
\end{itemize}

\begin{warnbox}
\textbf{Bad posture habits} can cause:
\begin{itemize}[leftmargin=*, itemsep=2pt]
  \item Back and neck pain
  \item Headaches from eye strain
  \item Wrist pain (from typing at wrong angles)
  \item Tiredness and difficulty concentrating
\end{itemize}
Always sit up straight and take a short break every 30 minutes!
\end{warnbox}

\begin{warnbox}
Never force-pull cables or plug in USB drives the wrong way around --- always check the connector shape first! Forcing a cable can damage both the cable and the port permanently.
\end{warnbox}

\bytetip[warn]{If something goes wrong --- a strange error, a weird noise, or a screen that won't turn on --- \textbf{don't panic!} Tell your teacher immediately. Trying to fix it yourself could make things worse.}

\begin{funfact}
Some schools in Japan have ``computer lab shoes'' --- students change into clean indoor slippers before entering the lab to keep dust and dirt away from the equipment. It might sound extreme, but dust is one of the biggest enemies of computers!
\end{funfact}
