\chapter{Keyboard and Mouse Skills}
\label{ch:keyboard-mouse}

\begin{objectives}
  \item Identify and name key groups on a keyboard
  \item Use the mouse confidently for all basic actions
  \item Understand ergonomic typing posture
  \item Practise touch-typing techniques
\end{objectives}

\bytetip[tip]{The keyboard and mouse are your main tools for communicating with the computer. Mastering them is like learning to ride a bicycle --- once you've got it, you've got it for life!}

\bytetip[fun]{The first computer mouse was invented in 1964 by \textbf{Douglas Engelbart}. It was made of wood and had only one button! It was called a ``mouse'' because the cable coming out of the back looked like a tail.}

\begin{funfact}
Modern optical mice use a tiny camera that takes up to \textbf{18,000 pictures per second} of the surface below to detect movement. That's faster than any video camera you've ever seen!
\end{funfact}

\begin{reviewqs}
  \item Name and describe five key groups on a keyboard.
  \item What is the difference between single-click and double-click?
  \item What key do you press to type capital letters temporarily?
  \item Name the three main parts of a mouse.
  \item What does the scroll wheel do?
\end{reviewqs}

\begin{challenge}
Time yourself typing a paragraph of 50 words using proper finger placement (don't look at the keyboard!). Record how long it took. Practise for a week and time yourself again. How much did you improve?
\end{challenge}
