\bytetip[fun]{Professional typists use shortcuts \emph{hundreds} of times per day. Over a year, that saves an estimated \textbf{8 full working days} of time! That's more than a week of extra holiday, just from pressing \key{Ctrl}+\key{S} instead of clicking menus.}

\begin{shortcutbox}
\textbf{Pro tip:} The most important shortcut to build as a habit is \key{Ctrl}+\key{S} (Save). Press it every few minutes while working --- it only takes a fraction of a second and could save you from losing hours of work if the power goes out!
\end{shortcutbox}

\begin{tryit}
Open Notepad and type a sentence. Now practise these shortcuts in order:
\begin{enumerate}[itemsep=2pt]
  \item \key{Ctrl}+\key{A} (select all text)
  \item \key{Ctrl}+\key{C} (copy it)
  \item Press \key{Enter} twice, then \key{Ctrl}+\key{V} (paste it)
  \item \key{Ctrl}+\key{Z} (undo the paste)
  \item \key{Ctrl}+\key{S} (save the file)
\end{enumerate}
You just used 5 shortcuts in under 10 seconds!
\end{tryit}

\begin{funfact}
The \key{Ctrl}+\key{C}, \key{Ctrl}+\key{X}, \key{Ctrl}+\key{V} shortcuts were popularised by Apple's original Macintosh in 1984 (using the Command key instead of Ctrl). They chose C for Copy, X for Cut (like scissors cutting), and V for Paste (because V looks like an insertion mark in proofreading).
\end{funfact}
