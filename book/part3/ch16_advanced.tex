\section{The IPO Cycle Revisited}
\label{sec:ipo-advanced}
\index{IPO cycle}

Every computer task follows the \textbf{Input--Process--Output} (IPO) cycle:

\begin{center}
\begin{tikzpicture}[node distance=1cm]
  \node[hwbox, fill=FunSky!15, minimum width=3cm] (input) {\faKeyboard\ \textbf{Input}\\Data goes in};
  \node[hwbox, fill=FunPurple!10, draw=FunPurple, minimum width=3cm, right=1.5cm of input] (process) {\faMicrochip\ \textbf{Process}\\CPU works on it};
  \node[hwbox, fill=LabGreen!10, draw=LabGreen, minimum width=3cm, right=1.5cm of process] (output) {\faDesktop\ \textbf{Output}\\Results shown};
  \draw[thickarrow] (input) -- (process);
  \draw[thickarrow] (process) -- (output);
  
  % Storage below
  \node[hwbox, fill=NoteOrange!10, draw=NoteOrange, minimum width=3cm, below=1cm of process] (storage) {\faDatabase\ \textbf{Storage}\\Data saved};
  \draw[NoteOrange, line width=1.5pt, <->] (process) -- (storage);
\end{tikzpicture}
\end{center}

\bytetip[tip]{Everything you do on a computer follows the IPO cycle. Typing a letter? Input = keystrokes, Process = converting to text, Output = letters on screen, Storage = saving the document!}

\begin{realworld}
When you search on Google: \textbf{Input} = your search query, \textbf{Process} = Google's servers scanning billions of pages, \textbf{Output} = the search results you see. This entire process takes less than one second!
\end{realworld}

\begin{funfact}
There are approximately \textbf{5.5 billion} internet users worldwide --- that's about 68\% of the entire human population! And the number is growing every day.
\end{funfact}
